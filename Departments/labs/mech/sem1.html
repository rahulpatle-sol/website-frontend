Applied Physics Lab -I

Physics Lab experiments:

  1. To measure length, radius of a given cylinder, a test tube, and a beaker using a Vernier caliper and find the volume of each object.
  2. To determine the diameter of a wire, a solid ball, and the thickness of cardboard using a screw gauge.
  3. To determine the radius of curvature of a convex and concave mirror/surface using a spherometer.
  4. To verify the triangle and parallelogram law of forces.
  5. To find the coefficient of friction between wood and glass using a horizontal board.
  6. To determine the force constant of a spring using Hook’s Law.
  7. To verify the law of conservation of mechanical energy (PE to KE).
  8. To find the moment of inertia of a flywheel.
  9. To find the viscosity of a given liquid (Glycerin) by Stoke’s law.
  10. To find the coefficient of linear expansion of the material of a rod.
  11. To determine atmospheric pressure at a place using Fortin’s barometer.
  12. To measure room temperature and the temperature of a hot bath using a mercury thermometer and convert it into different scales.

Applied Chemistry Lab -I

Chemistry experiments:

  1. Volumetric and Gravimetric analysis:
  2. Preparation of standard solution of oxalic acid or potassium permanganate.
  3. To determine strength of given sodium hydroxide solution by titrating against standard oxalic acid solution using phenolphthalein indicator.
  4. Standardization of KMnO4 solution using standard oxalic acid and determine the percentage of iron present in given Hematite ore by KMnO4 solution.
  5. Iodometric estimation of copper in the copper pyrite ore.
  6. Volumetric estimation of total acid number (TAN) of given oil.
  7. Volumetric estimation of:
  8. Total hardness of given water sample using standard EDTA solution.
  9. Alkalinity of given water sample using 0.01M sulphuric acid.
  10. Proximate analysis of coal:
  11. Gravimetric estimation of moisture in given coal sample.
  12. Gravimetric estimation of ash in given coal sample.

ENGINEERING GRAPHICS Lab

Experiments :

  1. Draw horizontal, vertical, 30-degree, 45-degree, 60-degree, and 75-degree lines, different types of lines, dimensioning styles using Tee and Set squares/drafter. (do this exercise in sketch book) - Part I - 02 hrs
  2. Write alphabets and numerical (Vertical only) (do this exercise in sketch book) - 02 hrs
  3. Draw regular geometric constructions and redraw the given figure (do this exercise in sketch book) - Part I - 02 hrs
  4. Draw regular geometric construction and redraw the given figure (do this exercise in sketch book) - Part II - 02 hrs
  5. Draw a problem on orthographic projections using the first angle method of projection having plain surfaces and slanting - Part I - 02 hrs
  6. Draw another problem on orthographic projections using the first angle method of projection having slanting surfaces with slots - Part II - 02 hrs
  7. Draw two problems on orthographic projections using the first angle method of projection having cylindrical surfaces and ribs - Part I - 02 hrs
  8. Draw two problems on isometric view of simple objects having plain and slanting surfaces by using natural scale - Part I - 02 hrs
  9. Draw two problems on isometric projection of simple objects having cylindrical surfaces by using isometric scale - Part I - 02 hrs
  10. Draw freehand sketches/conventional representation of machine elements in sketch book such as thread profiles, nuts, bolts, studs, set screws, washers, locking arrangements - Part I - 02 hrs
  11. Problem-based learning: Given the orthographic views of at least three objects with a few missing lines, the student will try to imagine the corresponding objects, complete the views, and draw these views in sketch book - Part I - 02 hrs
  12. Draw basic 2D entities like Rectangle, Rhombus, Polygon using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part I - 02 hrs
  13. Draw basic 2D entities like Circles, Arcs using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part II - 02 hrs
  14. Draw basic 2D entities like Circular and rectangular array using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part III - 02 hrs
  15. Draw blocks of 2D entities comprising Rectangle, Rhombus, Polygon, Circles, Arcs, circular and rectangular array bodies using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part IV - 02 hrs
  16. Draw basic 3D entities and their components in 2D using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part I - 02 hrs
  17. Draw basic 3D entities and their components in 2D using AutoCAD (Printout should be a part of progressive assessment) - Part II - 02 hrs
