Semester 4 Lab Guide

  • Construction Materials Lab
  • Basic Surveying Lab
  • Mechanics of Material Lab
  • Concrete Technology Lab
  • Geotechnical Engineering Lab

Construction Materials Lab

  • Identify various sizes of available coarse aggregates from a sample of 10 kg in the laboratory and prepare a report (60, 40, 20, 10 mm).
  • Prepare lime putty by mixing lime (1 kg) with water in appropriate proportion and prepare a report on the slaking of lime.
  • Identify various layers and types of soil in a foundation pit by visiting construction sites and prepare a report consisting of photographs and samples.
  • Select first-class, second-class, and third-class bricks from the stack of bricks and prepare a report based on their properties.
  • Measure dimensions of 10 bricks, find the average dimension and weight, and perform field tests—dropping, striking, and scratching by nail—correlating the results obtained.
  • Identify different types of flooring tiles such as:
    • Vitrified tiles
    • Ceramic tiles
    • Glazed tiles
    • Mosaic tiles
    • Anti-skid tiles
    • Chequered tiles
    • Paving blocks
    Prepare a report about their specifications.
  • Apply two or more coats of selected paint on the prepared base of a given wall surface for an area of 1m x 1m using a suitable brush or rollers while adopting safe practices.
  • Prepare cement mortar of proportion 1:3 or 1:6 using artificial sand as a specially processed construction material.

Basic Surveying Lab

  • Measure distance between two survey stations using chain, tape, and ranging rods when two stations are inter-visible.
  • Determine the area of an open field using chain and cross-staff survey.
  • Measure Fore Bearing and Back Bearing of survey lines of an open traverse using Prismatic Compass.
  • Measure Fore Bearing and Back Bearing of a closed traverse of 5 or 6 sides and correct the bearings and included angles for local attraction.
  • Undertake a Survey Project with chain and compass for a closed traverse with a minimum of 5 sides around a building.
  • Plot the traverse on an A1 size imperial drawing sheet for data collected in the Survey Project mentioned in practical No.6.
  • Undertake simple leveling using dumpy level/Auto level and leveling staff.
  • Undertake differential leveling and determine Reduced Levels by the Height of Instrument method and Rise and Fall method using dumpy level/Auto Level and leveling staff.
  • Undertake fly leveling with double check using dumpy level/Auto level and leveling staff.
  • Undertake a Survey Project with a Leveling instrument for Profile leveling and cross-sectioning for a road length of 500 m with cross-sections at 30 m intervals.
  • Plot the L-section with a minimum of 3 cross-sections on an A1 size imperial sheet for data collected in the Survey Project mentioned in practical No.11.
  • Undertake a Survey Project for plotting a contour map using the block contouring method for a block of 150m x 150m with a grid of 10m x 10m.
  • Plot the contours on an A1 size imperial drawing sheet for data collected in the Survey Project mentioned in practical No.13.
  • Measure the area of an irregular figure using a Digital Planimeter.

Mechanics of Material Lab

  • Study and understand the use and components of Universal Testing Machine (UTM).
  • Perform Tension test on mild steel as per IS:432(1).
  • Perform tension test on Tor steel as per IS:1608, IS:1139.
  • Conduct compression test on a sample test piece using a Compression Testing Machine.
  • Perform Single Shear and Double Shear test on any two metals, e.g., Mild steel, Brass, Aluminium, Copper, Cast Iron, etc., as per IS:5242.
  • Plot Shear Force and Bending Moment diagrams for cantilever and simply supported beams.
  • Plot Shear Force and Bending Moment diagrams for overhanging beams under different types of loads, including moment loading.
  • Conduct Flexural test on a timber beam with a rectangular section in both orientations as per IS:1708, IS:2408.

Concrete Technology Lab

  • Determine fineness of cement by Blaine’s air permeability apparatus or by sieving.
  • Determine standard consistency, initial and final setting times of cement.
  • Determine compressive strength of cement.
  • Determine silt content in sand.
  • Determine bulking of sand.
  • Determine water absorption of fine and coarse aggregates.
  • Determine fineness modulus of fine aggregate by sieve analysis.
  • Determine impact value of aggregate.
  • Determine crushing value of aggregate.
  • Determine abrasion value of aggregate.
  • Determine elongation and flakiness index of coarse aggregates.
  • Determine workability of concrete by slump cone test.
  • Prepare concrete mix of a particular grade and determine compressive strength of concrete for 7 and 28 days.
  • Demonstration of NDT (Non-Destructive Testing) equipment.

Geotechnical Engineering Lab

  • Identification of rocks from the given specimen.
  • Determine water content of a given soil sample by oven drying method as per IS: 2720 (Part-II).
  • Determine specific gravity of soil by pycnometer method as per IS 2720 (Part-III).
  • Determine dry unit weight of soil in the field by core cutter method as per IS 2720 (Part-XXIX).
  • Determine dry unit weight of soil in the field by sand replacement method as per IS 2720 (Part-XXVIII).
  • Determine Plastic and Liquid Limit along with Plasticity Index of a given soil sample as per IS 2720 (Part-V).
  • Determine Shrinkage limit of a given soil sample as per IS 2720 (Part-V).
  • Determine grain size distribution of a given soil sample by mechanical sieve analysis as per IS 2720 (Part-IV).
  • Determine coefficient of permeability by constant head test as per IS 2720 (Part-XVII).
  • Determine coefficient of permeability by falling head test as per IS 2720 (Part-XVII).
  • Determine shear strength of soil by direct shear test as per IS 2720 (Part-XIII).
  • Determine shear strength of soil by vane shear test as per IS 2720 (Part-XXX).
  • Determine MDD (Maximum Dry Density) and OMC (Optimum Moisture Content) by standard Proctor test of a given soil sample as per IS 2720 (Part-VII).
  • Determination of CBR (California Bearing Ratio) value on the field as per IS 2720 (Part-XVI).
