Hydraulics Lab

Welcome to the Hydraulics Lab! This course helps students understand fluid flow parameters and hydrostatic pressure.

  • Use piezometer to measure pressure at a given point.
  • Use Bourdon’s Gauge to measure pressure at a given point.
  • Use U tube differential manometer to measure pressure difference between two given points.
  • Find the resultant pressure and its position for a given situation of liquid in a tank.
  • Use Reynold’s apparatus to determine type of flow.
  • Use Bernoulli’s apparatus to apply Bernoulli’s theorem to get total energy line for a flow in a closed conduit of varying cross sections.
  • Use Friction factor Apparatus to determine friction factor for a given pipe.
  • Determine minor losses in pipe fittings due to sudden contraction and sudden enlargement.
  • Determine minor losses in pipe fitting due to Bend and Elbow.
  • Calibrate Venturi meter to find out the discharge in a pipe.
  • Calibrate the Orifice to find out the discharge through a tank.
  • Use Current meter to measure the velocity of flow of water in an open channel.
  • Use Pitot tube to measure the velocity of flow of water in an open channel.
  • Use triangular notch to measure the discharge through an open channel.
  • Use Rectangular notch to measure the discharge through an open channel.
  • Determine the efficiency of a centrifugal pump.

Advanced Surveying Lab

Welcome to the Advanced Surveying Lab! This course covers plane surveying, Theodolite surveying, and modern surveying techniques.

  • Use plane table survey to prepare plans of a plot of seven-sided closed traverse by Radiation Method.
  • Use plane table survey to prepare plans, locate details by Intersection Method.
  • Use plane table survey to prepare plans, locate details by Traversing Method.
  • Use plane table survey to carry out a Survey Project for a closed traverse for a minimum of five sides around a building.
  • Use transit Theodolite to measure Horizontal and Vertical angles by Direct Method.
  • Plot the traverse on an A1 size imperial drawing sheet for the collected data from the preceding Theodolite Survey Project.
  • Use Theodolite as a Tacheometer to compute reduced levels and horizontal distances.
  • Set out a circular curve by Rankine’s Method of Deflection Angles.
  • Use micro-optic Theodolite to measure Horizontal angles by Direct Method.
  • Use EDM to measure horizontal distance.
  • Use Total Station instrument to measure horizontal distances.
  • Use Total Station instrument to measure vertical angles.
  • Use Total Station instrument to carry out a Survey Project for a closed traverse for a minimum of five sides.
  • Plot the traverse on an A1 size imperial drawing sheet for the collected data from the preceding Total Station Survey Project.
  • Use GPS to locate the coordinates of a station.

Building Planning and Drawing Lab

Welcome to the Building Planning and Drawing Lab! This course focuses on planning principles, graphical representation, and CAD-based drawings.

  • Draw various types of lines, graphical symbols for materials, doors, windows, sanitary, water supply, and electrical installations.
  • Write a summary of observations from a given architectural drawing.
  • Measure the units of an existing building and draw a line plan.
  • Prepare line plans for residential and public buildings.
  • Draw submission drawings for a single-storey load-bearing residential building.
  • Draw submission drawings for a framed structure residential building.
  • Prepare working drawings using CAD software.
  • Draw two-point perspective drawings for small objects.

Major Project

Students will work on a project related to civil engineering concepts, applying their skills in a real-world scenario.

  • Selection of project topic based on industrial needs.
  • Preparation of project proposal and design specifications.
  • Implementation of the proposed design.
  • Testing and performance evaluation of the developed project.
  • Presentation and documentation of project findings.

Seminar

Students will conduct a seminar on contemporary civil engineering topics to enhance their knowledge and communication skills.

  • Selection of seminar topic based on emerging technologies.
  • Research and collection of relevant information.
  • Preparation of presentation materials.
  • Delivery of seminar with interactive discussions.
  • Submission of a detailed report on the seminar topic.
